MAE 510 Wind Energy Conversion

The course on wind energy conversion immediately captured my attention as I embarked on my academic journey. Motivated by a profound desire to contribute to a greener and cleaner planet, my choices have always gravitated towards the exploration of renewable energy. Under the expert guidance of Dr. John Hall, the course commenced; however, unforeseen circumstances later led us to continue with Dr. Teng Wu. Half of our grade depended on the final project, while the rest comprised innovative assignments that broke away from conventional teaching methods. Our inaugural session delved into a team discussion about a patent dispute over the Haliade - X12 MW -14 MW offshore wind turbine's design. Another notable assignment combined reading scholarly articles with virtual tours of topics such as smart microgrids, next-gen wind turbine blades, and digital twins. The Wind Data Assignment was a standout experience. We analysed global wind data from NREL spanning three consecutive years, leading to in-depth visual interpretations of wind speeds and power densities.
Our final project centred on "Assessing the Viability of Toroidal Blades in Wind Turbines." This intricate exploration required a grasp of Froudeâ€™s Momentum Theory, Wake effect analysis, Weibull Distribution, and Power Production Analysis. We conducted a comparative study between the Toroidal and conventional turbines. Employing tools like Simscale, our team performed rigorous CFD Analysis to deepen our insights. Our findings indicated the superior efficiency of conventional wind turbine blades over their toroidal counterparts. Yet, the unique noise-reducing attributes of toroidal blades offer potential advantages in noise-sensitive regions. Given the nascent stage of toroidal blade design, further R&D could bridge the efficiency gap, elevating their role in the wind energy domain.
Reflecting on this educational journey, I am immensely thankful for the insights gained and the expansion of my knowledge base in wind turbine designs. I wholeheartedly recommend this enriching course to anyone aspiring to specialize in renewable energy.
